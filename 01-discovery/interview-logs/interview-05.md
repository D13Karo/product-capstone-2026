## The Story

Mamuka is the kind of person who takes charge because nobody else will. He started organizing the KIU informal football group in his second year because "nobody else was doing it and games kept falling apart." He has been doing it for two years and is visibly tired of it.

He was blunt and efficient in the interview. He answered questions precisely and didn't over-explain. When asked about his biggest frustration, he didn't hesitate: "I spend more time communicating about the game than actually playing it."

Here is his weekly coordination routine, which he described without prompting: Post the schedule on Facebook (5–10 min). Repost it in Messenger because people don't check Facebook (5 min). Send individual DMs to the 6–8 core players who he knows won't see either post (15–20 min). Field questions from people asking about the same info he already posted (ongoing, 20–30 min total). When something changes, repeat the entire cycle.

"Last month I changed the game time by one hour. I posted it everywhere. Three people still showed up at the wrong time and blamed me." He paused. "I don't blame them. The system is broken."

He has considered quitting as organizer. "The football is great. The admin work is not." He estimated he spends 45–60 minutes per week just managing communications for a 60-minute game.

---

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "I post it on Facebook, then Messenger, then DM the core guys individually. Three steps for one piece of information. Every single time."

2. "I spend more time communicating about the game than actually playing it. That's backwards."

3. "When something changes, I have to announce it in four places and people STILL show up to the wrong location. At some point it stops being my fault."

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 5 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** He is actively considering quitting as organizer — the coordination overhead has become unsustainable. 45–60 min/week on logistics for a 60-minute game is objectively disproportionate. This is the highest-stakes form of the problem.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Mamuka is the human infrastructure holding the entire group together, and the coordination burden is so high that losing him would collapse the group — the problem is one burnout away from eliminating the sports activity entirely.

---

## Current Workaround

- Posts to Facebook, then reposts to Messenger, then sends individual DMs
- Pins the schedule post in Messenger (doesn't help — pins get ignored)
- Created a simple spreadsheet once (abandoned — nobody else used it)

**Why it's inadequate:** Each channel reaches a different subset of people, so nothing reaches everyone. Pinned posts are ignored. The spreadsheet required everyone to change their behavior, which they didn't.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Organizer perspective reveals the problem has a supply side (coordination cost) not just a demand side (information access)
- His spreadsheet attempt and its failure is directly relevant to product direction (voluntary tool adoption is hard)
- He said unprompted: "If something just auto-sent a reminder to everyone without me doing anything, that would change my life"

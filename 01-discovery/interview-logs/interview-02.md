## The Story

Gega is a regular in a 5-on-5 football group at KIU. The group is organized across two platforms: a Messenger group for casual chat and a Facebook group where official tournament brackets are sometimes posted. He described the information as "split between two places, and neither one is up to date."

His most frustrating experience was during a weekend mini-tournament in March. The bracket was posted on Facebook but the venue change was only posted in Messenger. He saw the Facebook post and went to the listed venue. Six of the planned 10 players did the same thing. The game was delayed 45 minutes while everyone figured out what was happening. "We wasted almost an hour just getting organized," he said.

He also described the problem from a competitive standpoint: he plays to win, and showing up late or flustered affects his performance. He said he'd rather skip a game than "show up stressed and not knowing what's going on."

He has tried to take ownership — he created a separate WhatsApp group for just the core 8 players to share "the real info" — but now there are three parallel channels and it's still confusing.

## One-Sentence Insight

Gega has tried to solve the fragmentation himself by creating a sub-group, which only added another layer to the problem — showing that the information architecture itself is broken, not just individual habits.

---

## Current Workaround

- Checks both Facebook group and Messenger chat before every game
- Created a WhatsApp sub-group with 8 core players
- Calls the organizer directly if he can't find confirmation within 10 minutes

**Why it's inadequate:** Three channels means triple the checking, and the organizer is not always reachable by phone. Confirmation calls are awkward and slow.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- The split between Facebook (for "official" announcements) and Messenger (for day-to-day updates) is a pattern that came up spontaneously, not from prompting
- He mentioned that newer players (freshmen) are most disadvantaged because they don't know which channel to trust and don't have the social capital to call the organizer directly

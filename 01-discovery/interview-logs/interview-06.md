## The Story

Nata is the most organized person in the interview set. She schedules everything, keeps color-coded notes, and visibly bristled when describing how disorganized the sports communication is. "It offends me, honestly," she said, and she was only half joking.

She has developed the most elaborate personal coping system of any interviewee: a personal notes app where she manually logs game times, locations, and any changes she spots in the chats. She screenshots announcements and creates her own calendar events. This takes her about 10–15 minutes per week just for basketball coordination.

Despite all this effort, she still showed up to the wrong venue once. "I had screenshotted the location, I had it in my notes, and someone changed the venue in a chat message I never saw because it was posted at 11pm." She was not angry — she was resigned. "The system is broken and I cannot fix it on my own."

She is particularly frustrated by the lack of accountability: when information is wrong, nobody owns it. "If I message the organizer and say 'this is wrong,' he says 'I posted it.' If I message the chat, nobody responds. There's no source of truth."

She suggested, very specifically, something like "a single Google Doc that everyone can see but only the organizer can edit." She's essentially describing a pinned, updatable information source with controlled permissions.

---

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "I screenshot every game announcement and put it in my notes. I still showed up to the wrong venue. Screenshots become outdated the moment something changes."

2. "There is no source of truth. Everyone has a different version of the information and nobody knows whose version is right."

3. "I need a place where the organizer posts once and everyone sees the same thing. Not four group chats with different updates."

## One-Sentence Insight

Nata is doing more organizational work than anyone else just to consume information, and the fact that her meticulous system still fails her proves the problem is structural, not behavioral.

---

## Current Workaround

- Screenshots all announcements into a notes app
- Creates personal calendar events for each game
- Logs changes manually when she catches them in the chat

**Why it's inadequate:** Screenshots capture a moment in time; when the information changes, the screenshot is immediately obsolete and she has no way to know.

## Interview Log 02: The Failure of the "Power User"

### 1. Metadata
* **Date:** March 28, 2026
* **Duration:** 29 minutes
* **Interviewer:** Mariam Tskhomelidze
* **Interviewee Pseudonym:** Nata (KIU-CS-06)
* **Recruitment:** Reached out via Google Meet; recommended as a "highly organized" player.

### 2. ICP Verification
* **Profile:** 3rd-year CS student, highly organized.
* **Evidence:** Plays basketball twice weekly. Uses a personal notes app and calendar specifically for sports.
* **Pain Point:** Despite a meticulous manual system, she still experiences "wrong venue" errors.
* **ICP Match:** **Confirmed.** She is the "Extreme User."

### 3. Verbatim Quotes
> "It offends me, honestly [the disorganization]."
>
> "I screenshot every game announcement and put it in my notes... Screenshots become outdated the moment something changes."
>
> "There is no source of truth. Everyone has a different version of the information."
>
> "I need a place where the organizer posts once and everyone sees the same thing."
>
> "The system is broken and I cannot fix it on my own."

### 4. Problem Exploration Narrative
Nata provides the "structural" proof of our problem. Even as a "power user" spending 15 minutes a week manually syncing data, she fails due to real-time volatility. She articulated a **"Single Source of Truth" (SSOT)** requirement. Her frustration stems from a lack of accountability in buried chat histories. She is looking for a permission-based system rather than a collaborative one.

### 5. Mom Test Compliance Check
* **Pass:** I avoided pitching her the solution. Instead, I asked "How much time do you spend on those notes?" This gave me a **measurable metric** (15 min/week) of pain intensity.
* **Fail:** I almost asked if a "Google Doc" would fix it, which is a leading question.
* **Correction:** I should have asked: "What happened the last time you tried to use a shared document for this?" to see if she had already explored that path.

### 6. Personal Reflection
Nata’s interview was a "lightbulb moment." It showed me that the "Source of Truth" is the core value proposition. This interview moved our project from a "nice-to-have" to a **structural necessity**.

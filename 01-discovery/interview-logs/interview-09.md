## The Story

Tiko is intense. She leans forward (even over video call), answers questions before you finish asking them, and has opinions about everything. She does not tolerate inefficiency and the current sports coordination system has been, in her words, "an embarrassment."

She plays to win — she tracks her own stats mentally, she knows the score of every game she's been in this semester, and she references specific matches by date. When we asked about her most frustrating experience with sports communication, she didn't need a moment to think.

In February, a tournament bracket was posted — she was seeded and ready. The bracket changed the next day because one team dropped out and she found out 30 minutes before the revised kickoff. "I had already mentally prepared for one game. My warmup was calibrated for that opponent. Then I find out the schedule is completely different and I have five minutes to adjust?" She was angry retelling it.

She has zero patience for the "that's just how it is" attitude. "Everyone acts like this is normal. It's not normal. It's just what we've accepted because nobody has fixed it." She mentioned she has complained about this in the sports group chat directly and was essentially ignored.

She is the user most likely to switch to a better tool immediately if one existed, and most likely to advocate loudly for it.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 5 / 5

## One-Sentence Insight

Tiko is the ideal early adopter — high pain, high motivation, and explicitly willing to both switch and evangelize — but she needs a product that matches her intensity and reliability expectations.

## Current Workaround

- Checks the group chat multiple times per day during tournament weeks
- Screenshots the bracket and reads it obsessively
- Directly messages the tournament organizer before every game to confirm

**Why it's inadequate:** Even obsessive checking doesn't catch changes that happen between checks. Organizer DMs work but create overhead for both parties.

## Unexpected Findings

- She explicitly said she would evangelize a better solution — this is a distribution insight, not just a pain signal
- Her complaint being ignored in the group chat shows there is no feedback loop in the current system
- She described mental preparation as part of sports participation — suggesting that late information has a performance cost beyond just attendance

## The Story

Giorgi plays basketball with a group of about 20 students that rotates weekly. The games are announced in a Messenger group, but Giorgi has the Messenger app on silent because "the group never stops buzzing." He described an incident in late February where his team played a match short one player (himself) because the time moved up by one hour and he didn't see the notification until it was already over.

"I found out I had missed the game when someone sent me a screenshot of the final score in a separate DM," he said. He felt embarrassed and his team lost by 3 points — a result he attributes partially to being a man down.

Giorgi is generally the type of person who would prefer to receive a single clear notification rather than be part of a group chat. He compared his ideal system to "a calendar event with a reminder," noting that Google Calendar actually sends him reminders for his classes. "Why can't sports work like that?" was his specific framing.

He also noted he doesn't feel ownership over the problem — he just tries to check the chat before he leaves for the court, but sometimes forgets.

---

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "I muted the group because it was too noisy. Then I missed the time change. You can't win either way."

2. "My team played without me because of a one-hour shift. We lost. I felt terrible about it for two days."

3. "Sports should send me a notification like a calendar event. Just one clear ping and I'll be there."

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** He missed an actual game, his team lost, and he felt personal guilt. The notification problem caused him to mute the channel, which created a worse outcome — a feedback loop.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Giorgi muted the chat to reduce noise, which made him miss a time change — the current solution to information overload (muting) directly creates the problem of missed updates.

---

## Current Workaround

- Checks Messenger chat 1–2 times before leaving home on game day
- Asks a specific teammate to DM him if anything changes
- Sometimes sets a phone reminder to check the group

**Why it's inadequate:** He relies on a buddy system that breaks down when the buddy is also distracted. Manual reminders are easily skipped.

- The mute-then-miss loop is a key behavioral pattern worth surfacing in synthesis

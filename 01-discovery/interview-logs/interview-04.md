## The Story

Nia is one of those people who is everywhere at once. She knows everyone on campus and is part of at least five different sports-related group chats. She talked fast, jumped between stories, and kept saying "oh wait, and ALSO" — the interview ran long because she had so many examples.

Her problem is almost the opposite of Giorgi's: she's *too* engaged, not disengaged. She checks her chats constantly but there's so much volume that she still misses things. "I have notifications on for everything and I still miss stuff," she said. "It's like trying to drink from a firehose."

She described herself as a "social connector" — she's often the person who tells others about games. But she's been burned by passing along outdated information. "I told four people to come to volleyball on Saturday at 2pm. It got moved to 4pm. Nobody told me. I looked like such an idiot." She laughed while saying it but came back to it twice during the interview.

The funniest (and most revealing) moment: she pulled out her phone and showed us her Messenger. She had 12 unread chats, three of which were sports-related. While we were talking, a new message came in about a game this Thursday. "See? I don't even know if this is confirmed or just someone asking." She genuinely could not tell.

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "I have notifications on for everything and I STILL miss things. There's just too much happening everywhere."

2. "I'm the person who tells everyone about games, and I passed on wrong information because someone changed the time and didn't announce it properly. Four people showed up at the wrong time because of me."

3. "Look at this message — [shows phone] — is this confirmed? Is this a question? I cannot tell. This is my life every week."

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 3 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** She finds it annoying and socially embarrassing but she's resilient and treats it as normal friction. The problem doesn't stop her from participating — it just makes her the inadvertent spreader of misinformation. Her high social engagement amplifies the problem's reach.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Nia is a social hub for sports info and when she gets wrong information, the misinformation spreads to four or five additional people — making her a force multiplier for the problem.

---

## Current Workaround

- Has notifications enabled across all five chats
- Cross-references two chats before passing information to others
- Calls the organizer when she can't confirm

**Why it's inadequate:** Volume overwhelms the notification system even when it's fully enabled. Cross-referencing is unreliable because both chats may have different outdated information.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- She described herself as an information relay — her participation in the problem is *spreading* misinformation, not just experiencing it personally
- She showed a live example of an ambiguous message during the interview — very concrete
- She is actually doing more work than most (checking multiple chats) and still failing

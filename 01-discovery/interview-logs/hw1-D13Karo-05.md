## Interview Log 05: The Organizer’s Burnout

### 1. Metadata
* **Date:** March 26, 2026
* **Duration:** 35 minutes
* **Interviewer:** Davit Karoiani
* **Interviewee Pseudonym:** Mamuka (KIU-CS-05)
* **Recruitment:** Approached in the Building F dorm lounge; known as the primary organizer for KIU football.

### 2. ICP Verification
* **Profile:** 3rd-year CS student and Group Organizer.
* **Evidence:** Manages all logistics for the informal football group; has done so for two years.
* **Pain Point:** Spends 45-60 minutes of admin work for every 60-minute game.
* **ICP Match:** **Confirmed.** He represents the "Supply Side" of our problem—the person who creates the information.

### 3. Verbatim Quotes
> "I post it on Facebook, then Messenger, then DM the core guys individually. Three steps for one piece of information. Every single time."
>
> "I spend more time communicating about the game than actually playing it. That's backwards."
>
> "Three people still showed up at the wrong time and blamed me... I don't blame them. The system is broken."
>
> "The football is great. The admin work is not."
>
> "If something just auto-sent a reminder to everyone without me doing anything, that would change my life."

### 4. Problem Exploration Narrative
Mamuka provides a "high-stakes" perspective. While players are annoyed, organizers are **burning out**. He described a manual "syncing" process where he has to repeat the same data across Facebook, Messenger, and Direct Messages. 

The most revealing part of the narrative was his failed attempt at using a **spreadsheet**. He tried to centralize the info, but players refused to leave their chat apps to check it. This teaches us that for any solution to work, it must be as low-friction as a Messenger notification, but as organized as a database. Mamuka is currently the "human glue" holding the sports community together; if he quits, the games stop.

### 5. Mom Test Compliance Check
* **Pass:** I asked him to walk me through his "weekly coordination routine." This allowed him to list his manual steps (FB -> Messenger -> DMs) without me suggesting them. This gave me an **objective time estimate** (45-60 min/week).
* **Fail:** At the end, I didn't push back enough when he said "if something auto-sent a reminder... that would change my life." This was a "compliment" or "feature request" which is usually fool's gold in the Mom Test.
* **Correction:** I should have asked: "Have you tried setting up automated reminders using any other tool before?" to see if he had actually tried to automate his life or if he was just dreaming.

### 6. Personal Reflection
This interview shifted my focus from the "consumer" to the "provider." I realized that if we don't solve the organizer's pain, there will be no games for the players to attend. Mamuka's exhaustion was palpable. It made me realize that **automation** isn't just a "cool feature"—it's a retention strategy for the people who actually make sports happen on campus.

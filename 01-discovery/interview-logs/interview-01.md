## The Story

Khatia plays football with a mixed group of KIU students roughly twice a week. The games are organized through a Messenger group chat called "KIU Ball" with about 35 people. She described a specific incident in February where a game she had planned to attend for three days was silently moved from the main field to a smaller courtyard. The organizer posted the change in the chat, but by the time Khatia saw it, 40 messages had been sent after it. She showed up at the original field, waited 15 minutes, then walked to the other location arriving 20 minutes late.

She said this wasn't unusual — she described the group chat as "basically a news feed that I have to scroll through every time I want to know what's actually happening." She checks the chat an average of 4–5 times per day during active tournament periods.

She also mentioned that she once recruited two friends to come watch a game, and the game got cancelled an hour before. "I was the one who looked stupid in front of them," she said. After that incident she stopped inviting people.

---

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "The chat has 35 people and everyone talks in it about everything — football is maybe 20% of what gets posted. Finding the actual schedule update means scrolling past memes and voice notes."

2. "I showed up to the wrong field and there was nobody there. I felt like an idiot. It had been posted but I missed it."

3. "I stopped telling friends about games because I'm embarrassed when things change and I've already invited them. It's easier to just go alone."

---

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** She has changed her social behavior (stopped inviting friends) and actively wastes time doing manual chat monitoring. The problem is frequent (multiple times per month) and has real social consequences.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Khatia is not just missing games — she is actively limiting her social circle around sports because she cannot trust the information channel.

---

## Current Workaround

- Scrolls through "KIU Ball" chat 4–5 times per day
- Texts one specific friend (the de facto organizer) directly to confirm details
- Screenshots game info so she can reference it offline

**Why it's inadequate:** The direct-text workaround only works if that one person responds; during busy periods he doesn't always answer quickly. Screenshots become outdated when details change.

## Unexpected Findings

- She is not just a participant — she occasionally coordinates the women's side of mixed games and faces the same problem from the organizer's perspective
- She mentioned a specific person ("Sandro") who is the informal hub of information, and "if Sandro doesn't post, nobody knows anything"

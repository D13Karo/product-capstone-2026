## The Story

Misho is the most laid-back person we interviewed. He shrugged frequently, spoke slowly, and seemed genuinely unbothered by most things. When asked if he found the sports communication frustrating, he said: "I mean... yeah? Kind of? It's whatever."

But here's the thing — even Misho has been burned. He missed an entire game in January because a post about a rescheduled time was buried under a cascade of memes and voice notes. He found out when he saw a photo from the game in someone's story. "I was a little annoyed," he said. Coming from Misho, "a little annoyed" is roughly equivalent to anyone else saying "I was furious."

He said he doesn't check the group chat proactively — he waits for something to show up in his notifications. "If it doesn't ping me, I don't go looking." He acknowledged this means he often gets the information late, but he seemed to view it as a cost of doing business. "Sports are supposed to be relaxed, not another thing I have to manage."

The most revealing moment was when we asked what would make things better. He said: "Honestly? Just one message, directly to me, that says 'game is Saturday at 3pm, field B.' Done. I don't want to join a group. I don't want a schedule. Just ping me." 

He paused and added: "Why is that so hard?"

---

## 3 Key Quotes

1. "I'm not going to scroll through 200 messages to find out when the game is. If it's not at the top, I'm not going to see it."

2. "I found out I missed the game from someone's Instagram story. That's how I get my sports news, apparently."

3. "Just ping me directly. Game time, location, done. Why is that so hard?"

---

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 2 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** He is low-engagement and accepts some friction as normal. The problem doesn't consistently stop him from participating — he just misses some games and doesn't stress about it. However, even at his level of acceptance, he has a clear ideal and would use a better system if it required zero effort from him.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Even the most disengaged participant has a clear mental model of what good looks like — a single direct notification — and his effortlessness threshold defines the minimum viable experience: zero-effort information delivery.

---

## Current Workaround

- Relies entirely on push notifications from Messenger
- Does not proactively check the group
- Uses Instagram stories as a secondary source (passive)

**Why it's inadequate:** Notifications from a busy group chat are noisy; the important information gets buried in the notification stream just as it does in the chat.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Misho represents an important user type: the low-motivation participant who will only engage if friction is near zero — this sets the floor for design
- His "ping me directly" model is almost exactly a push notification to a dedicated app or bot
- Even at low pain intensity, he articulated the solution more clearly than most high-pain interviewees

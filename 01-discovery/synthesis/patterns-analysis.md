### Pattern 1: Schedule Changes Not Received in Time (P2)

**Intensity Rating:** ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (5/5 - Severe Pain) — Average: 4.1/5

**Emotional Language Used:**
- Interview #03 (Giorgi): "I muted the group because it was too noisy. Then I missed the time change. You can't win either way."
- Interview #06 (Nata): "Venue change posted at 11pm — I saw the screenshot I had, not the update — showed up at wrong place"
- Interview #09 (Tiko): "I had mentally prepared for one opponent. The schedule changed. I had five minutes to adjust."

**Evidence of Impact:**
- **Tangible loss:** "Game moved one hour earlier — posted in chat — I didn't see it — team played without me" (I-03, Giorgi)
- **Stress caused:** "Late updates affect preparation, not just attendance" (I-09, Tiko)
- **Negative emotions:** "I would rather skip a game than show up stressed and not knowing what's happening" (I-02, Gega)
- **Behavioral impact:** "I found out I missed the game from a score screenshot in a DM" (I-07, Misho)

**Quantified Impact Metrics:**
- Frequency: Multiple missed/disrupted games reported across 9/10 interviews
- Self-reported pain average: 4.1 / 5 across all interviewees

**Is this "hair on fire"?:** ☑ Yes

**Explanation:** This pattern produces the most concrete, irreversible loss — a player misses the game entirely. The pain is not abstract friction; it is a wasted journey, a missed match, or a tournament bracket discovered 30 minutes before kickoff. It scores the highest average pain of all patterns.

### Pattern 2: Information Fragmented Across Platforms (P1)

**Intensity Rating:** ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (4/5 - Major Pain) — Average: 4.0/5

**Emotional Language Used:**
- Interview #06 (Nata): "There is no source of truth. Everyone has a different version of the information."
- Interview #02 (Gega): "There's a Messenger group, a Facebook group, and now a WhatsApp sub-group — three places, one game"
- Interview #01 (Khatia): "I text my teammate to get the 'real' information because group chats aren't trustworthy"

**Evidence of Impact:**
- **Time wasted:** "I check both platforms before every game because I don't know which one has the update" (I-02, Gega)
- **Stress caused:** "I screenshot announcements to have them offline but screenshots become wrong the moment something changes" (I-06, Nata)
- **Negative emotions:** "I have 12 unread sports chats and still miss things" (I-04, Nia)
- **Behavioral impact:** "Two solutions I built failed because I couldn't control where the organizer puts information" (I-10, Cotne)

**Quantified Impact Metrics:**
- Frequency: Present in 100% of interviews — 10/10
- Self-reported pain average: 4.0 / 5

**Is this "hair on fire"?:** ☑ Yes

**Explanation:** Universal presence across all 10 interviews. Even technically capable users (Cotne) couldn't solve it — the fragmentation is structural, not behavioral. The inability to trust any single channel creates ongoing cognitive overhead before every single game.

### Pattern 3: Organizer Coordination Burden Is Unsustainable (P3)

**Intensity Rating:** ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (4/5 - Major Pain for organizers) — Average: 2.9/5 overall, 4.5/5 for organizer-adjacent users

**Emotional Language Used:**

- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "I've considered quitting as organizer. The football is great. The admin is not."
- Interview #10 (Cotne): "The organizer has to want to use the tool. If they won't, nothing else matters."

**Evidence of Impact:**
- **Time wasted:** "I spend 45–60 minutes per week managing communications for a 60-minute game" (I-05, Mamuka)
- **Stress caused:** "When something changes, I repeat the whole announcement cycle — it never gets shorter" (I-05, Mamuka)
- **Behavioral impact:** "Sandro is graduating in June — without him the group will lose its coordinator" (I-10, Cotne)
- **Near-term risk:** A specific, named coordinator is leaving in June 2026 — the group has no succession plan

**Quantified Impact Metrics:**
- Time: 45–60 minutes per week for a 60-minute game (1:1 ratio of admin to play)
- Frequency: Every game requires a full re-announcement cycle
- Self-reported pain: 4–5/5 for Mamuka and Cotne; lower for passive participants who don't see the organizer side

**Is this "hair on fire"?:** ☑ Yes (for organizers specifically)

**Explanation:** The overall average (2.9) is suppressed because passive participants don't feel this pain directly. But this pattern is the root cause of P1 and P2 — organizer overload is why fragmentation exists and why updates get lost. Solving P3 cascades to fix the others.

## Root Cause Analysis

### Pattern 2: Schedule Changes Not Received in Time

**Surface Problem:** Players miss game time or venue changes posted in group chats.

1. **Why does this happen?**
   → Because change announcements get buried in high-volume group chats.

2. **Why does THAT happen?**
   → Because change notifications must compete with off-topic messages in the same channel.

3. **Why does THAT happen?**
   → Because there is no distinction between "important update" and "casual chat" — all messages look identical.

4. **Why does THAT happen?**
   → Because the communication infrastructure (Messenger, Facebook groups) was not designed for time-sensitive event coordination.

5. **Why does THAT happen?**
   → Because there is no purpose-built sports coordination tool for informal student groups — only general-purpose social apps fill the gap.

**ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFIED:** General-purpose chat apps cannot prioritize critical event updates over casual conversation, so time-sensitive changes are indistinguishable from noise and go unseen.

**Is this a root cause or symptom?**
☑ Root cause (explains multiple surface problems)

**Evidence from Interviews:**
- Interview #03 (Giorgi): "I muted the group because it was too noisy. Then I missed the time change. You can't win either way."
- Interview #01 (Khatia): "The delay between posting and seeing can be hours. By then the window to respond is gone."
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "I posted the change in four places and three people still showed up to the wrong location"

**Surface problems this root cause explains:**
1. Players arriving at wrong venue
2. Players missing games entirely due to time changes
3. Tournament changes discovered only minutes before kickoff

### Pattern 1: Information Fragmented Across Platforms

**Surface Problem:** Students don't know which platform has the correct, current game information.

1. **Why?** → Organizers post across Facebook, Messenger, and WhatsApp with no single source.
2. **Why?** → Because organizers use multiple platforms to reach different subsets of participants.
3. **Why?** → Because no single platform has adoption from all participants — so organizers must duplicate.
4. **Why?** → Because participants are distributed across platforms with no shared default.
5. **Why?** → Because there is no purpose-built coordination tool for informal student sports — only general-purpose social apps exist.

**ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFIED:** The absence of a purpose-built sports coordination channel forces organizers into multi-platform broadcasting, which creates fragmentation, inconsistency, and high overhead.

**Is this a root cause or symptom?**
☑ Root cause (explains multiple surface problems)

**Evidence from Interviews:**
- Interview #06 (Nata): "There is no source of truth. Everyone has a different version of the information."
- Interview #10 (Cotne): "Two solutions I built failed because I couldn't control where the organizer puts information"
- Interview #02 (Gega): "The Facebook post is 'official' but updates only come in Messenger"

**Surface problems this root cause explains:**
1. Players checking multiple platforms before every game
2. Screenshots going stale when information changes
3. Organizer spending time on redundant announcements across channels

### Pattern 3: Organizer Coordination Burden Is Unsustainable

**Surface Problem:** Organizing a 60-minute game requires 45–60 minutes of communication work per week.

1. **Why?** → The organizer must post to 3+ platforms and send individual DMs to ensure coverage.
2. **Why?** → Because no single channel reaches all participants reliably.
3. **Why?** → Because participants use different platforms and check them at different frequencies.
4. **Why?** → Because there is no push-based, centralized notification mechanism the organizer can use once to reach everyone.
5. **Why?** → Because no such tool exists for informal student sports groups — the organizer manually compensates for missing infrastructure.

**ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFIED:** Organizers compensate for fragmented participant attention by doing redundant work. With no scalable, low-effort broadcast tool, coordination burden grows linearly with group size and change frequency.

**Is this a root cause or symptom?**
☑ Root cause (explains multiple surface problems)

**Evidence from Interviews:**
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "Post on Facebook → repost in Messenger → DM core players. Three steps, every time, for every announcement."
- Interview #10 (Cotne): "Any solution needs to require zero extra work from the organizer"
- Interview #01 (Khatia): "Sandro is the hub for everything — if he doesn't post, nothing works"

**Surface problems this root cause explains:**
1. Organizer considering quitting due to admin overhead
2. Updates still missing people even after being posted in 4 places
3. Entire group at risk when a single coordinator graduates

## Top 3 Validated Patterns for Problem Statement

### 1️⃣ PRIMARY PATTERN: Schedule Changes Lost in Chat Noise (P2)
- **Frequency:** 9/10 interviews
- **Intensity:** 4.1/5 (highest average)
- **Root Cause:** General-purpose chat apps cannot distinguish critical updates from casual conversation
- **Why this is #1:** Highest average pain intensity. Produces the most concrete, irreversible loss — a missed game. Present in nearly every interview. The structural nature of the root cause (wrong tool for the job) means no behavioral change will fix it.

### 2️⃣ SECONDARY PATTERN: Fragmented Information — No Single Source of Truth (P1)
- **Frequency:** 10/10 interviews
- **Intensity:** 4.0/5
- **Root Cause:** No purpose-built coordination channel forces organizers into multi-platform broadcasting
- **Why this is #2:** Universal frequency — the only pattern present in all 10 interviews. Directly enables P2 (updates get lost because they're spread across platforms). Cannot be solved by participants — only addressable at the organizer/infrastructure level.

### 3️⃣ TERTIARY PATTERN: Organizer Is the Single Point of Failure (P3)
- **Frequency:** 7/10 interviews
- **Intensity:** 4.5/5 for organizer-adjacent users
- **Root Cause:** No low-effort broadcast tool exists, so organizers manually compensate with redundant multi-platform work
- **Why this is #3:** This is the causal root that generates P1 and P2. A solution that doesn't address organizer burden will fail — as demonstrated by every failed workaround in the data (Cotne's bot, Gega's sub-group, Mamuka's pinned message). Additionally, a near-term event (Sandro graduating in June 2026) makes this urgent.

## Product Direction Implications

**Our solution must address:**
1. **PRIMARY:** Provide a single source of truth — one place where the schedule lives and only the organizer can update it
2. **PRIMARY:** Push change notifications automatically — when anything changes, all participants receive a direct alert without the organizer re-announcing
3. **CRITICAL CONSTRAINT:** Require near-zero extra effort from the organizer — Cotne's Telegram bot proved that even a working tool fails if it requires the organizer to change behavior

**What this means for solution design:**
- Push notifications are non-negotiable — pull-based solutions (check a website, open an app) will fail
- The organizer is the primary user, not the participant — design for organizer ease first
- RSVP / attendance signaling would address P5 downstream — if organizer knows 6/8 are confirmed, they can act before turnout failure
- Distinguish confirmed information from casual chat — remove ambiguity entirely

**What we should NOT focus on:**
- Another general-purpose group chat — this is what already exists and fails
- Participant-side tools that require checking in — the evidence shows participants will not consistently pull information
- Features that require organizer to post in multiple places — this is the exact behavior causing the problem

---

## Decision & Rationale

**DECISION:** We will focus our product solution on addressing **schedule change delivery and information fragmentation (P1 + P2)**, with the organizer coordination burden (P3) as the primary design constraint.

**RATIONALE:**
P2 and P1 are co-primary patterns — present in 9–10/10 interviews with average pain of 4.0–4.1/5. Both share the same root cause: the absence of a purpose-built coordination channel forces informal sports groups to use general-purpose social apps that cannot prioritize critical event information. P3 is the causal root that generates P1 and P2 — every failed workaround in our data broke at the organizer layer, confirming that any solution which adds friction for the organizer will not be adopted. By designing a low-effort, push-based tool that requires one action from the organizer to reach all participants, we address all three validated patterns simultaneously and prevent the downstream community decay (P5) that follows repeated coordination failures.

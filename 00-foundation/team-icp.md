## Primary ICP Profile 1: The Participant

### 1. Segment

**Who is this person?**
KIU student (18–24) who participates regularly in informal peer-organized sports events — football, basketball, futsal, or volleyball. Socially active but not in a leadership or organizing role. Relies on Facebook groups and Messenger chats for game updates and schedule information.

**Validated by:** 10/10 interviews — all interviewees matched this profile  
**Test:** Look for students on campus sports fields or in student common areas checking group chats before a game.

---

### 2. Context

**When and where does the problem occur?**
Throughout the academic semester, in the 12–48 hours before each game, when last-minute schedule changes are most likely and least likely to be seen in busy group chats.

**Evidence:**
- Interview #03 (Giorgi): "Game moved one hour earlier — posted in chat — I didn't see it — team played without me"
- Interview #08 (Nana): "Venue change posted at 11pm — participants who were already asleep missed it"

---

### 3. Primary Pain

Participants miss game time or location changes, arrive at wrong venues, or show up to cancelled events — because critical updates are buried in high-volume group chats with no way to distinguish a cancellation from casual conversation.

**Evidence:**
- Interview #06 (Nata): "There is no source of truth. Everyone has a different version of the information."
- Interview #03 (Giorgi): "I muted the group because it was too noisy. Then I missed the time change. You can't win either way."
- Interview #01 (Khatia): "I stopped inviting friends because I'm embarrassed when things change after I've invited them"

**Validation rate:** 9/10 interviews confirmed missing or nearly missing a game due to info failure

---

### 4. Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It | Why It Fails |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| Screenshot announcements | I-06 (Nata) | Goes stale the moment info changes |
| Check multiple platforms before every game | I-02 (Gega) | Time-consuming; still misses updates |
| Mute noisy group chats | I-03 (Giorgi) | Causes missed updates entirely |
| Text organizer directly | I-01 (Khatia), I-07 (Misho) | Doesn't scale; puts burden on organizer |
| Create WhatsApp sub-group | I-02 (Gega) | Added a third channel, made fragmentation worse |

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When a game time or location changes, I want to be notified immediately and directly, so I can show up at the right place at the right time without having to monitor multiple group chats."

**Evidence:** Interview #07 (Misho): "Just ping me directly — game time, location, done. Why is that so hard?"

---

### 6. Success Signal

- No missed games due to information failure
- No need to check multiple platforms before each game
- Confidence that showing up is worth the effort

**Evidence:** Interview #09 (Tiko): "If someone built a proper system, I would switch immediately and make everyone else switch too"

---

### 7. Pain Intensity

**Average:** 4.0 / 5.0 across 10 interviews  
**Range:** 2/5 (Misho — low-effort user) to 5/5 (Gega, Mamuka, Tiko)  
**Emotional language observed:** "frustrated", "embarrassed", "you can't win either way", "I would rather skip than show up stressed"

---

### 8. Where to Find Them

- Facebook groups for KIU sports communities
- Messenger and WhatsApp group chats for informal sports events
- On-campus sports fields and student common areas

## Primary ICP Profile 2: The Organizer

> **New profile added in v3.0** — entirely absent from v1.0 and v2.0. Discovered through interviews and identified as the most critical design target.

### 1. Segment

**Who is this person?**
A KIU student who voluntarily coordinates informal sports events for a peer group — managing schedules, venues, player availability, and communications. Not officially appointed; takes on the role out of social motivation. Typically the single point of contact for all game logistics.

**Validated by:** I-05 (Mamuka) directly; I-01 (Khatia), I-10 (Cotne) referenced their organizer (Sandro) extensively  
**Test:** There is typically one per sports group — find them by asking "who sets up your games?"

---

### 2. Context

**When and where does the problem occur?**
Every game cycle — when coordinating availability, booking venues, announcing times, and re-announcing every time something changes. The burden compounds when last-minute changes require a full re-announcement across 3+ platforms.

**Evidence:**
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "Post on Facebook → repost in Messenger → DM core players. Three steps, every time, for every announcement."
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "When something changes, I repeat the whole announcement cycle — it never gets shorter"

---

### 3. Primary Pain

Organizers spend as much time on logistics as on playing — manually broadcasting across multiple platforms, repeating announcements for every change, and still failing to reach everyone. The burden is unsustainable and at least one organizer is considering quitting.

**Evidence:**
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "I spend more time communicating about the game than actually playing it"
- Interview #05 (Mamuka): "I've considered quitting as organizer. The football is great. The admin is not."
- Interview #10 (Cotne): "Sandro is graduating in June — without him the group will lose its coordinator"

**Quantified impact:** 45–60 minutes per week on logistics for a 60-minute game (1:1 ratio)

---

### 4. Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Why It Fails |
|-----------|--------------|
| Post across Facebook + Messenger + WhatsApp | Creates fragmentation; still misses people |
| DM core players individually | Time-consuming; doesn't scale |
| Pin announcements in group chat | Gets buried under casual conversation |
| Ask participants to confirm attendance manually | Low response rate; organizer still can't predict quorum |

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When I organize a game, I want to post once and have everyone receive it directly, so I can spend my time playing instead of managing communications."

**Evidence:** Interview #05 (Mamuka): "If auto-reminders existed, I would switch immediately"

---

### 6. Success Signal

- One action to reach all participants
- No re-announcement cycle when things change
- Able to see who has confirmed attendance before the game
- Successor can take over the organizer role without friction

---

### 7. Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4.5 / 5.0 for organizer-adjacent users (I-05, I-10)  
**Emotional language observed:** "considering quitting", "the admin is not worth it", "I can't control where the organizer puts information"

---

### 8. Where to Find Them

- One per informal sports group — ask any participant "who organizes your games?"
- Often reachable through the same Facebook groups and Messenger chats they manage

---

### 9. Why Organizers Are the Primary Design Target

Every failed workaround in our data broke at the organizer layer:
- Cotne's Telegram bot failed because the organizer wouldn't switch platforms (I-10)
- Gega's WhatsApp sub-group made fragmentation worse because the organizer still posted in the original channels (I-02)
- Participant-side fixes cannot solve an organizer-side infrastructure problem

**If the organizer doesn't adopt the solution, participants cannot benefit — regardless of how good the solution is.**

**Evidence:** Interview #10 (Cotne): "Any solution needs to require zero extra work from the organizer"

## Scoring Reference

For each filter, score 1 to 3:

- **3 = Clear evidence this passes**  
- **2 = Possible pass, but uncertain — needs more information**  
- **1 = Fails this filter**

| Filter | What it measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| Real and Recurring | Happens often enough to justify building a solution |
| Painful Enough to Act | People are already trying to solve it (workarounds exist) |
| Underserved Today | Existing solutions are genuinely inadequate |
| Accessible to You | You can interview users this week and build for this space |

## Evaluations

---

### Problem 1: No tracking of sports tournaments

**Real and Recurring**  
Score: 2/3  
Justification: Happens regularly during the semester, but only for students actively involved in sports.

**Painful Enough to Act**  
Score: 2/3  
Known workaround: Students check Facebook groups or rely on friends.  
Justification: Users care but do not actively try to solve it beyond basic effort.

**Underserved Today**  
Score: 3/3  
Existing solutions: Facebook groups and Messenger chats.  
Justification: These are unstructured and unreliable, no centralized system exists.

**Accessible to You**  
Score: 3/3  
Who could you interview this week: Friends, classmates, student athletes  
Justification: Easy access to this group within university.

**Total:** 10/12  
**Recommendation:** Possible candidate  

---

### Problem 2: LMS notifications unreliable

**Real and Recurring**  
Score: 3/3  
Justification: Happens frequently across multiple courses, especially during busy weeks.

**Painful Enough to Act**  
Score: 3/3  
Known workaround: Students manually check LMS or rely on classmates.  
Justification: Students actively try to avoid missing updates, showing strong pain.

**Underserved Today**  
Score: 3/3  
Existing solutions: LMS platforms with weak notification systems.  
Justification: Current systems are unreliable and fragmented.

**Accessible to You**  
Score: 3/3  
Who could you interview this week: KIU students, classmates, friends  
Justification: Very easy to reach and interview.

**Total:** 12/12  
**Recommendation:** Strong candidate  

---

### Problem 3: Guitarists struggle with melody creation

**Real and Recurring**  
Score: 2/3  
Justification: Occurs during practice, but only for a niche group of users.

**Painful Enough to Act**  
Score: 1/3  
Known workaround: Trial-and-error playing.  
Justification: Users accept this as part of learning rather than actively solving it.

**Underserved Today**  
Score: 2/3  
Existing solutions: Tutorials, music theory apps.  
Justification: Some solutions exist, but not perfectly tailored.

**Accessible to You**  
Score: 1/3  
Who could you interview this week: Few musician friends  
Justification: Hard to find enough relevant users quickly.

**Total:** 6/12  
**Recommendation:** Drop

## Decision

**Our committed problem for Lab 2 is:**  
LMS notifications unreliable (students miss important updates and deadlines)

**This problem was chosen because:**  
It scored highest across all four filters, affects a large number of students, and has a direct impact on academic performance, time, and stress levels.

**The runner-up problem we are keeping as secondary ICP candidate is:**  
No tracking of sports tournaments and events

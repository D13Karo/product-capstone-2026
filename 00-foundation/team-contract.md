## Communication
 
- **Primary channel:** Messenger group
- **Standup cadence:** Twice weekly, evenings, voice call
- **Response time:** Relaxed style, but all important messages acknowledged within the same day
- **Meeting location:** Online via call (Messenger or similar)
 
**If someone goes silent for 24+ hours with a deadline approaching:** The team will first attempt to contact the person directly. If no response, the Program Lead will follow up and reassign tasks as necessary. Repeated cases may be escalated to the instructor.

## Decision-Making
 
| Scope | Process |
|---|---|
| Day-to-day (no impact on direction) | Any two team members can decide |
| Product direction (what we build, who we target) | Simple majority vote; Program Lead holds tiebreaker |
| Major pivots (changing core problem or abandoning significant work) | Full team discussion, minimum 24-hour deliberation, then majority vote |
 
**Documentation:** All significant product decisions are recorded in a `decisions/` note in the repository with the date, options considered, and reasoning.
 
---
 
## Conflict Resolution
 
The team follows the **KIU 4-Step Conflict Protocol:**
 
1. **Verbal Discussion (15 min)** — Each party states their position once, clearly and completely. Interrupting is not permitted.
2. **Written View (10 min)** — Each party writes a brief summary of their position and the core reason they hold it.
3. **Cool-off (24 hours)** — No decision is made immediately.
4. **Program Lead Decision** — If still unresolved, the Program Lead makes the final call.
 
**Escalation to instructor:** [zeshan.ahmad@kiu.edu.ge](mailto:zeshan.ahmad@kiu.edu.ge)

## Contribution and Accountability
 
- **Tracking:** All contributions tracked via GitHub commits and pull requests. Documents, interview notes, and decisions must be committed by the person responsible.
- **Lab attendance:** Flexible, but members must inform others in advance if they cannot attend.
- **Deadlines:** The Program Lead ensures final submission. All members must complete their tasks at least **12 hours before deadlines**.
- **Consistent under-contribution:** After two documented instances, the team will redistribute work and notify the instructor. Peer assessment will reflect actual contribution.
 
---
 
## Team Norms
 
- Write clear and meaningful commit messages (no `update` or `fix` alone).
- Communicate before making major changes to shared work or direction.
- Give honest, constructive feedback during reviews.
- If blocked, inform the team within 24 hours.
- Prioritize finishing tasks over perfection when deadlines are close.
